


The Do's (Build Your Brand)
Audit your "Public" self. Periodically search your name in an incognito window to see what a recruiter sees.
Network with intent. Follow industry leaders, join professional groups, and use LinkedIn to bridge the gap between "student" and "pro."
Give credit where it’s due. If you share a cool graphic, a quote, or a project, always tag the original creator. Digital integrity is huge.
Use "Privacy" as a tool, not a shield. Keep your personal life private, but ensure your public presence reflects your actual skills and values.
Fact-check before you click 'Share.' As a student, your credibility is your currency. Spreading misinformation, even accidentally, hurts your reputation.

The Don’ts (Protect Your Future)
Don’t post in the "Heat of the Moment." If you’re angry at a professor or a peer, wait 24 hours. The internet is permanent; your mood isn't.
Don’t overshare your location. Avoid tagging your exact current spot. It’s a safety risk and invites unnecessary "digital tailing."
Don’t engage with "Rage-Bait." Avoid getting into toxic comment wars. It rarely changes minds and often leaves a trail of unprofessionalism.
Don’t ignore the "Screenshot Rule." Never post anything—even in a DM or private story—that you wouldn’t want on the front page of a news site.
Don’t trash-talk your institution. Complaining about "boring" classes is one thing; attacking the integrity of your college or workplace is a career-killer.
